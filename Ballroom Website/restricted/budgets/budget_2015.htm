Section III - Club Purpose

RPI Ballroom Dance provides students with a unique dance experience through lessons, workshops, social dances, and competition opportunities, as well as a sense of belonging to a community of student dancers dedicated to having fun and developing abilities.

Section IV - Current Club Activities

RPI Ballroom is composed of three lesson tracks (Argentine Tango, Ballroom/Latin, and Lindy Hop) and a competitive collegiate DanceSport team. All activities across these four groups fall under three pillars: instruction, competition, and community.


INSTRUCTION
Common to all four groups is frequently reoccurring professional instruction. In addition to regularly scheduled instruction, we occasionally hold special workshop events that can span a day to a whole weekend.

COMPETITION
Through the Ballroom Team, students attend collegiate ballroom competitions that regularly draw several hundred dancers from across the Northeast. The Ballroom Team officially travels to three regional competitions per semester. In the spring semester, RPI Ballroom hosts one of these major regional competitions on campus. While the Ballroom Team mostly engages in competitive activity, the students from the Lindy Hop lesson track also have opportunity to compete at the annual Boston Tea Party swing event in March.

COMMUNITY
Given the social nature of dancing, building community among our members is a natural and highly important cause. We try to foster community through our informal practice sessions and special events (see listing below). Additionally, we organize social dancing trips to events across the Capital District like USA Dance and the First Friday Capital Swing Dance.


SPECIAL EVENTS:

Program 3: Ballroom Team

In addition to the technique lessons listed below, the ballroom dance team receives American Style lessons for free from a volunteer from Capital District dance community. 

We are continuing to request funds to purchase reference books for American Style Bronze and Silver Syllabus. These books offer a comprehensive reference for all competition-legal dance figures within each level, providing Team members the means of correctly self-guiding their improvement and development as dancers.
We are also requesting funding for 20 small group lessons which allows one small group lesson per partnership. Each partnership on the Team would be entitled to one of these lessons per semester and would be required to pay 50% of the costs when they take the lesson, if a couple decides not to make use of their small group lesson, they will not have to pay and neither will the Student Union. Small group lessons are not intended to be taken by a single partnership at one time, rather by multiple couples of similar skill level in a small group. As a result, an individual partnership will be able to take multiple small group lessons, receiving the benefits of the increased personal attention which is not possible in a large group lesson. This is one area that would greatly benefit Team members, especially the higher level dancers, many of whom are approaching the level of technique knowledge of our volunteer instructor.  As a result, current lessons do not allow higher-level dancers to meet their full potential. In the past partnerships have paid for private lessons on their own, but these are very expensive and by receiving a 50% subsidy, a greater proportion of the Ballroom Dance Team will be able to excel and better represent RPI at the competitions the Team attends. This will also open the door for new members to join that are put off by the large upfront cost of advancing to a high level of dance.

Program 4: Workshops: Ballroom Team (American Style)

Each semester we will offer two workshops in American Style. We will both offer one Silver and one Bronze level group lesson, and up to 10 small group lessons. The participants in the workshop pay for the group lessons (the Participation Fee) which more than covers all costs for the group lessons. After the group lessons, there are slots of time available where small group lessons (4-8 people) are offered. Those participating in the small group lessons are responsible for covering half the cost of the lesson through the Small Group Lesson Fee and the instructors are only paid for the time slots actually used.

During the normal course of a semester the lessons offered by a professional instructor are the Technique lessons which are covered by Team dues and are not level specific. Offering this separated lessons allows those with the ability to pay to have more targeted instruction towards improving their abilities.

This is also one of the few times were our upper-level dancers would be able to receive instruction at their level without increasing costs to everyone on the Team.

Program 5: Workshops: Ballroom Team (International Style)

Each semester we will offer two workshops in International Style: one at the bronze level, one at the silver level, as well as up to 8 small group lessons. The participants in the workshop will pay for the group lessons (the Participation Fee) which more than covers the costs for the group lessons. After the group lessons, there would be slots of time available where small group lessons (4-8 people) would be offered. Those participating in the small group lessons would be responsible for covering half the cost of the lesson, and we will only pay the instructors for the time slots actually used. 

During the normal course of a semester team members receive no instruction in International dance styles, due to the lack of a regular International style instructor. International style dances compose one half of the dances offered at each competition we attend, and team members regularly dance International style dances in competition without formal instruction. These workshops will be used as a way to motivate and direct the self study of International technique and figures which team members already engage in. These workshops are critical to placement and advancement of team members in International dance styles.

GENERAL COMPETITION BUDGETING GUIDELINES AND NOTES:
1. Registration fees are expressed per person. Competitors may choose to dance in as many events as allowed within the fee.

2. Due to the growing number of Team members and the limited number of members with vehicles, the Team may require multiple van rentals for a competition to allow all interested Team members to attend. These are listed at a subsidy of $155, based on the weekend trip rate stated in the 2013-2015 Budgeting Guidelines and Procedures.

3. Team members pay a total of 60% of trip fees (lodging, registration fees, gas & tolls, transportation, and any associated fees excluding meals); these are split among all Team members attending the competition. This is based on the competition travel guidelines outlined in the 2013-2015 Budgeting Guidelines and Procedures.

4. When possible, the Ballroom Team tries to stay at the homes of Team members in the surrounding location of the competition. Alternatively, some competition hosts can offer free housing for a portion of the Team.

5. Team members attending a competition generally provide transportation with personal vehicles. This reduces costs and allows greater flexibility in arriving and leaving competition venues.

================================

Program 5: Live Swing Dance

The Lindy Hop group holds one major dance per year. These are meant to provide students with an exciting opportunity to practice what they have learned throughout the semester. Dances also allow for interaction between the beginner and intermediate level dancers, as well as interaction with dancers from the Capital Region. In the end, social swing dances are tremendous opportunities to enhance abilities while having a wonderful experience. As always, we will provide a free introductory swing dancing lesson before our dances to attract more people and support interested students.

This major headline live band dance is a key part of the swing dance experience at RPI. In 2010, RPI Ballroom hosted The Boilermaker Jazz Band, an extremely popular swing dance band that performed for a routine on Dancing with the Stars. In 2011, we brought Gordon Au & His Grand Street Stompers. These dances were extremely successful, and brought many students and Capital Region dancers together. Future bands will be determined based on band availability. Preferred venues include Academy Hall Auditorium, Academy Hall SAR, or Mother's.

This program is accessory to the swing club's core goals and will require a subsidy of ~$1,445. It has not been run in the past two years and is being included for historical purposes. If funds are needed to run it again, we will write a proposal.

We have demand for additional instruction across all four groups, especially the Ballroom Team. Our existing workshops and dances are well-attended and foster the drive many of our members have to improve. Programs relating to the Ballroom Team target a major outstanding issue with our competitiveness against other schools. In spite of a great demand for instruction at higher levels (Bronze and Silver American and International Styles), the current Ballroom Team operation does not target this issue. As a result, other schools consistently perform better than us at collegiate DanceSport competitions in Bronze and Silver levels. See Appendix for priorities of Programs Items from Swing, Tango, Club, and Team.

1) Goal A: Maintain High Quality of Instruction

The core goal of the ballroom organization is to introduce RPI students to various forms of self-expression through dance. We also aim to foster involvement and community with various dance communities throughout the Capital District and the Northeast. See Appendix for priorities of Programs Items from Swing, Tango, Club, and Team.

2) Goal D: Compete with Regional Collegiate Ballroom Teams

The RPI Ballroom Team has been competing in collegiate Dancesport competitions for more than twenty years. The primary goal of the Ballroom Team is to offer the level of instruction required to competitively participate in Dancesport competitions against other area schools. See Appendix for priorities of Programs Items from Swing, Tango, Club, and Team.

2) Goal C: Foster Community Within RPI Ballroom

We strive to develop a community of dancers at RPI and become a part of the larger dance community in the Captial District, rather than simply to provide access to affordable dance classes. We have incorporated parties/social gatherings into the Lesson Tracks towards the goal of increasing social interactions within the organization and with members of the community. See Appendix for priorities of Programs Items from Swing, Tango, Club, and Team.

3) Goal E: Expand Membership

Ballroom dancing is inherently an extremely social engagement. We constantly aim to expand our community and offer experiences to our members and non-dancers at RPI. See Appendix for priorities of Programs Items from Swing, Tango, Club, and Team.

4) Goal F: Engage RPI with Special Events

RPI Ballroom is responsible for several additions to the culture and student life at RPI including: an annual Halloween dance party, a major live band swing dance, and GM Week "Dancing with the RPI Stars" events. See Appendix for priorities of Programs Items from Swing, Tango, Club, and Team.

5) Goal G: Expand Scope and Size of RPI Ballroom Dance Competition

The RPI Ballroom Dance Competition is one of the Northeast's collegiate DanceSport competitions. The competition attracts more than a hundred dancers from across the region and last year marked the formation of a fruitful partnership with USA Dance in running our competition. We plan to more actively incorporate the Captial District dance community and dancers from other schools in our competition by organizing social dance and banquet. See Appendix for priorities of Programs Items from Swing, Tango, Club, and Team.

Appendix

Please note that the dues structure of previous budgets tended to overestimate membership numbers because of the historic overlap in membership between team and club. The membership system is being overhauled with this budget to better account for this and the fact that most team members tend to pay lesson-track dues anyways. Starting with this budget, team members are required to pay lesson track dues in order to join team, to better leverage and utilize the lessons already offered by the lesson tracks. Please note that even with the change in membership structure and the more accurate membership projection, we are projecting approximately equal income from membership dues.

Please also note the the increase in requested subsidy is mostly due to a price increase in our instructors, accounting for ~$1100 of difference from last year's budget. We have made an effort to reduce costs in other programs to account for this difference and keep our subsidy request the same.
